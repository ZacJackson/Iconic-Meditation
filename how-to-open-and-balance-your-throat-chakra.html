How to open and balance your throat chakra

By Zac Jackson

throat Chakra vishudda

  • Element: Ether
  • Color: blue
  • Location: throat region (neck)
  • Attributes: The throat chakra is one of the most important energy centres in our body. It represents how we speak and listen to others, it also dictates how open or closed off you are from the world around you.
  • Imbalance: An imbalanced or blocked throat chakra will manifest as lack of creativity, not expressing what you think or feel because there's no point in doing so if people don't like it or want to hear it.

The throat Chakra: What is it?

The throat chakra is the fifth of seven major energy centers in the body. Also known as the vishudda which when translated means the “the purifier”.

A balanced throat chakra can mean self-expression without fear of rejection, openness to new ideas and experiences, being able to communicate your thoughts and feelings clearly which leads to a healthy self-esteem.

Everyday we are faced with challenges that can be draining or difficult to deal with alone, but by opening up about our struggles and talking through them, we find strength beyond ourselves. We gain wisdom from others' experiences and advice which helps us find peace no matter how chaotic life gets.

The throat chakra is an important energy center that can be blocked by a variety of things such as anger, sorrow, and fear. If your throat chakra is blocked it can lead to feeling frustrated, sad or angry. The good news is that you have the power to unblock your throat chakra through self-love and meditation.

Meditation and H-A-M Chanting to balance the throat Chakra

Opening and balancing this chakra means letting go of feelings of inadequacy and developing and building your self-esteem. It requires that you begin to put down insecurities you have about yourself.

When this centre is underactive or blocked we may find it difficult to express ourselves verbally or creatively; but when it’s open we can be very expressive people.

  1. Sit in a comfortable position (prefereably the lotus or the half lotus postition) with your back straight. Take a deep breath in then recite the mantra "I am an assertive and expressive individual".
  2. Notice the thoughts that come and go. Let them drift into the void of nothingness. Refrain from attaching to them, just notice and accept that they are there. Notice the way you feel. Don't become attached to the feeling. Just notice the feeling and accept that it is a part of you.
  3. Bring your attention to your throat region where your throat chakra is located. Pay attention to the physical sensation around that area. As you take another deep breath in, exhale with a deep long 'H-a-a-a-a-m' chant. This vibration will stimualte the throat region. Exhale til you feel comfortable.
  4. Each time you perform this chant imagine that you are letting go of feelings of inadequacy with every exhale and that you are breathing in positive pranic energy to that region with every inhale.
  5. Repeat this for up to five minutes a day until you start to notice changes in the way you hold attitudes towards other people and their negative and/or unpleasant traits

Keeping your throat chakra balanced

Once the throat chakra has become unblocked and energy is flowing through it freely it is possible for the flow of that energy to become imbalanced. A number of factors can cause this imbalance to take place such as an unhealthy diet, or poor regulation of your emotions. Opening the throat chakra does not mean that it will stay open. It must be tended to like a garden and be nurtured and constantly taken care of in order to ensure that an imbalance doesnt occur causing it to close.

I highly recommend incorporating this style of meditation into your life if you haven't already. If you want to relax with the power of music and meditation, then give this technique a try. It's been proven that listening to certain types of music can change your mood in just minutes.
