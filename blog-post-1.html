Mindfulness meditation: A comprehensive guide.

By Zac Jackson

Meditation 101: Getting to know the basics.

Comfortability

Sit in a seated position with your legs crossed or both feet flat on the floor. This will help to prevent you from falling asleep and with keeping your spine in an erect position (if that is possible). If you have issues with keeping your spine straight then it is also ok to sit or lie in a position where you feel as comfortable as you can.

key points

  • Wear comfortable clothing.
  • Sit in a comfortable position.

Environment

Find a comfortable place to sit where you won’t be distracted. And bare in mind that thoughts and physical sensations, such as an itch can be distracting. Overtime as you get better at meditating small distractions become anchors which will help you to progress with the practice of meditation.

Overtime as you begin to master mindfulness meditation you will find it easier to meditate in, and around the presence of noise. Remember that you have an internal environment as well as an external environment. Our aim is to work on our internal environment in order for our mind’s to change the way we interact with the external environment.

key points

  • Sit somewhere that helps you to relax
  • Sit where distractions are minimal.
  • Your internal environment determines your external one.

Breathing

Take a deep breath in slowly through your nostrils until you feel your diaphragm fill with oxygen (prana). Slowly exhale through your mouth. Let out as much air as you can but do not force it.

Let it flow naturally. Repeat this exercise 3-5 times until you feel relaxed. Once you have completed 3-5 cycles allow yourself to breathe normally. You can achieve this by bringing your attention away from the breath and into the mind to observe.

Notice how certain thoughts influence the rate at which you breathe. Some increase your heart rate and others slow it down. Stay focused on observing thoughts and feelings rather then attaching to them. Breathe naturally and try to stay relaxed.

Come back to the breath when your mind drifts. Utilize deep breathing to bring your self into a relaxed state and back into the present moment. A calm mind produces positive results.

key points

  • Breathe all the way in until your diaphragm fills with air (prana) then exhale through your nose.
  • Breathe deeply until your mind is relaxed then start to let your breathing happen naturally.
  • Stay focused on breathing as you inhale and exhale.
  • Focus on your breath which will help you to be present and in the moment.


Focus

Pay very close attention to your breath as you inhale and exhale. Notice the air going in and out. As you draw your attention towards yourself breathing notice the thoughts coming into the forefront of your mind. Pay attention to how these thoughts make you feel.

Acknowledge your emotional state. Let them arise then subside. Refrain from attaching to any thought that causes your emotions to charge. Just observe and become aware of the patterns in your mind.

Notice any physical sensation that occurs. If you need to scratch and itch then feel free to scratch the itch. Alternatively, you may let the sensation go away at its own will. Be mindful that it takes practice to achieve that level of awareness.

key points

  • Your mind will drift countless times. Keep coming back to the present moment by focusing on your breath (or something that your senses are picking up).
  • Observe your thoughts, emotions and physical sensations. Notice the patterns in your mind regarding thoughts and emotions.
  • Observe thoughts rather then attaching to, and thinking about them.


Time management

Whether it’s for ten seconds or 10 hours, being mindful will always bring you into the present moment away from the habit of drifting.

Trust the process and try not attach to an outcome. Mindfulness can be practiced anytime and anywhere. Consistency is the key so regular practice, even a few minutes a day can have a profound impact on your life.

With time comes growth. Your mind and body have been through a lot in the past. Be kind to yourself. It is very easy to become frustrated and give up.

Start with small 5-10 second bouts of mental silence over 3-5 minute periods throughout your day. Do this at least 2-3 times a day until you’re ready to take it further.

  • Keep bringing your focus back to your breathing whenever your mind drifts.
  • Practice for 3-5minutes a day until you are comfortable extending the time limit.
  • Be consistent and you will reap the reward of a more fulfilling and meaningful life.
