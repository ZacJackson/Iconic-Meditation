How to open and balance your sacral chakra

By Zac Jackson

Sacral Chakra Svadhishthana

  • Element: Water
  • Color: Orange
  • Location: Sacral region (Navel area)
  • Attributes: Known to govern emotional states, creativity, sensitivity, sexual pleasure, intimacy in relationships, emotional well-being, and self-expression.

The Sacral Chakra: What is it?

The sacral chakra is the second of seven major energy centers in the body. Also known as the Svadhisthana which when translated means the “dwelling place of the self”.

It governs our creative expression, sexuality and relationships. This is where we feel joy, pleasure and connection to others.

Our creativity can be expressed through playfulness or by creating something new such as a painting or recipe.

Everybody has this chakra but it will be stronger for some than for others depending on what stage of life they are at. In addition, its potency also depends on factors such as diet, exercise and the way you manage your thought and emotonal processes.

For example a teenager might not yet have strong connections with other people so their sacral chakra will be less developed than someone who has just started dating someone new.

The sacral chakra also rules our sexual organs so when it is out of balance it can lead to sexual dysfunction, depression, fearfulness or a lack of creativity.

Meditation and Vam Chanting to balance the Sacral Chakra

Opening and balancing this chakra means letting go of negative thought patterns that create excessive feelings of resentment and bitterness. Overtime you will begin to feel a greater sense of belonging and connectedness to the world around you and those residing in it along side of you.

Because this area is known to govern our emotional states, balancing the energy in this area will help you to become more tolerant of offences and less susceptible to mood swings and extreme outbursts of anger. You will not become distraught when dealing with the emotion of grief and the loss of loved ones because you will be more in touch with your emotional being.

  1. Sit in a comfortable position (prefereably the lotus or the half lotus postition) with your back straight. Take a deep breath in then recite the mantra "I am an assertive and expressive individual".
  2. Notice the thoughts that come and go. Let them drift into the void of nothingness. Refrain from attaching to them, just notice and accept that they are there. Notice the way you feel. Don't become attached to the feeling. Just notice the feeling and accept that it is a part of you.
  3. Bring your attention to your navel region where your throat chakra is located. Pay attention to the physical sensation around that area. As you take another deep breath in, exhale with a deep long 'V-a-a-a-a-m' chant. This vibration will stimualte the sacral region. Exhale til you feel comfortable.
  4. Each time your perform this chant imagine that you are letting go of negative energy from that area with every exhale and that you are breathing in pranic energy to that region with every inhale.
  5. Repeat this for up to five minutes a day until you start to notice changes in your ability to expres yourself and be assertive.

Keeping your sacral chakra balanced

Once the scaral chakra has become unblocked and energy is flowing through it freely it is possible for the flow of that energy to become imbalanced. A number of factors can cause this imbalance to take place such as an unhealthy diet, or poor regulation of your emotions. Opening the sacral chakra does not mean that it will stay open. It must be tended to like a garden and be nurtured and constantly taken care of in order to ensure that an imbalance doesnt occur causing it to close.
