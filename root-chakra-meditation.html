How to open your Muladhara aka Root Chakra

By Zac Jackson

Creating a solid foundation for your life.

How can you feel safe, stable, and grounded when the world around you feels unpredictable? It sounds like a paradox with no solution. But it doesn't have to be that way!

The root chakra is our solid foundation for balance in life; without it opened we will struggle to make any progress on the journey upwards.

In Eastern philosophies, this energy center is said to be located at the very bottom of the spine and coils around all other 7 chakras even though they are not in physical alignment.

Balancing your root chakra creates a new sense of faith in life's upsets so that they don't bring you down and make you feel discouraged and unwilling to live life completely.

Opening your root chakra with meditation

The root chakra is the first of 7 major chakras in our body, and it's all about grounding. It's also called the base or foundation, and it's responsible for keeping us grounded to Mother Earth.

Many of us are not feeling grounded and full of energy. We may be struggling to feel calm, or we may be experiencing a low mood.

This is often because our root chakra is not open which means we don't have enough life force flowing through us. In this blog post I'm going to show you how to open your root chakra so that you can get more life force flowing through your body!

The root chakra is also the foundation of our energy system and governs how we receive energy from others.

When you have your root chakra open, you are in a place where you can really feel what's going on with other people.

You’re also more able to take care of yourself because it allows for self-honoring, self-love, and willingness to be alone in solitude.

  • Step one: The root chakra is located at your base of the spine and it affects our sense of personal power, security, grounding and physical energy.

    The first step in opening this chakra is to find a comfortable position on the floor with legs crossed as close to your body as possible.

    You should be cross-legged with both feet tucked under you (or one foot if that's uncomfortable).

    Place your hands palms up on either side of your hips and take 3 deep breaths focusing on letting go of negative thoughts from head to toe.

  • Step two: Turn your attention to the base of the spine. This is where your root chakra is located. Notice any tightness in around the base of your spine.

    Since the root chakra element is fire, imagine that a bright red glowing light like an ember from a fire is bringing warmth to your entire being. Hold the thought for as long as you can and when you drift just keeping coming back to that same thought.

    let go of any negative thoughts that envoke feelings of fear. Be present and try to stay focused on the base of your spine and the bright red glowing light.
  • Step three: Do this for as long as you like (preferably for at least five minutes), then open your eyes when you’re ready.
