How to open and balance your heart chakra

By Zac Jackson

Heart Chakra Anahata

  • Element: Air
  • Color: Green
  • Location: Heart region (chest area behind the rib cage)
  • Attributes: Known to govern emotional states and our ability to have intimate relationships with others. ALso governs empathy, compassion, commitiment and positive attitudes.
  • Imbalance: An imbalance can be caused by a lack of empathy and being able to connect with others outside of, or even in your immediate circle.

The heart Chakra: What is it?

The heart chakra is the fourth of seven major energy centers in the body. Also known as the Anahata which when translated means the “Unhurt”.

Feelings such as anger, jealousy, low self-esteem are all signs that your heart chakra might need some attention. Luckily there are many ways to help heal this area so you can feel more fulfilled and grounded in your life again!

Our ability to practice love can be expressed through the way we interact with the environment aroud us. Practising love doesn't just mean loving other people, it also means loving yourself and the world around you.

Everybody has this chakra but it will be stronger for some than for others depending on what stage of life they are at. In addition, its potency also depends on factors such as diet, exercise and the way you manage your thought and emotonal processes.

For example a teenager might not yet have strong connections with other people so their heart chakra will be less developed than someone who has just started dating someone new.

This chakra is a symbol of love and self-love. You are never alone in this world, even when you feel as if nobody cares about you or that no one understands your feelings. Let the heart center create space for healing while cleansing the negative energies away with meditation and breathing techniques

Meditation and Y-A-M Chanting to balance the Heart Chakra

Opening and balancing this chakra means letting go of negative thought patterns that create excessive feelings of resentment, jealousy, bitterness and malice. It means learning to make righteous judgements based on behaviour rather then factors such as the outter appearance or ethnicity.

Because this area is known to govern our emotional states, balancing the energy in this area will help you to become more tolerant of offences and less susceptible to mood swings and extreme outbursts of anger. You will not become distraught when dealing with the emotion of grief and the loss of loved ones because you will be more in touch with your emotional being.

  1. Sit in a comfortable position (prefereably the lotus or the half lotus postition) with your back straight. Take a deep breath in then recite the mantra "I am an assertive and expressive individual".
  2. Notice the thoughts that come and go. Let them drift into the void of nothingness. Refrain from attaching to them, just notice and accept that they are there. Notice the way you feel. Don't become attached to the feeling. Just notice the feeling and accept that it is a part of you.
  3. Bring your attention to your heart region where your heart chakra is located. Pay attention to the physical sensation around that area. As you take another deep breath in, exhale with a deep long 'Y-a-a-a-a-m' chant. This vibration will stimualte the heart region. Exhale til you feel comfortable.
  4. Each time you perform this chant imagine that you are letting go of resentment and bitterness towards others with every exhale and that you are breathing in positive pranic energy to that region with every inhale.
  5. Repeat this for up to five minutes a day until you start to notice changes in the way you hold attitudes towards other people and their negative and/or unpleasant traits

Keeping your heart chakra balanced

Once the heart chakra has become unblocked and energy is flowing through it freely it is possible for the flow of that energy to become imbalanced. A number of factors can cause this imbalance to take place such as an unhealthy diet, or poor regulation of your emotions. Opening the heart chakra does not mean that it will stay open. It must be tended to like a garden and be nurtured and constantly taken care of in order to ensure that an imbalance doesnt occur causing it to close.
