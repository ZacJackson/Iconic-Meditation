Mindfulness meditation: A comprehensive guide.

Meditation 101: Getting to know the basics.

By Zac Jackson

Comfortability

Sitting in a seated position with your legs crossed or both feet flat on the floor is always helpful when it comes to meditation. This will help to prevent you from falling asleep and keeping your spine straight. If you have issues with keeping your spine straight then it's also ok to sit or lie in a position where you feel comforatable.

key points

  • Wear comfortable clothing.
  • Sit in a comfortable position.

Environment

Finding a comfortable place to meditate is always helpful when starting out with meditation. If possible, I highly recommend finding a quiet spot out in nature or somewhere outside among the elements and the abundance of life that thrives outdoors.

As you begin to familiarize yourself with meditation you will gain an awareness of sounds and how they contribute to both distraction and growth. For exapmle, certain noises can make you feel highly irritable while others, like soft music or birds chirping can make you feel relaxed.

key points

  • Sit somewhere that helps you to relax and feel at peace.
  • Meditate in a place or at a time where distractions have been minimized.
  • Surround yourself with energies (i.e. natural elements, plants etc), objects and sounds (i.e. frequencies and vibrations) that help you to feel balanced and calm.

Breathing

Deep breathing is at the heart of meditation. Breathing exercises are designed to help our brain function at optimal performance. such exercises send oxygen to the brain and to your blood cells which help to lower stress levels.

Many practitioners have come and gone over the years and have emphasised the importance of deep pranic breathing. The term pranic breathing can be summed up as the breathing in of pure life force energy.

This is one of the main reasons that breathing is at the heart of meditation. Breathing helps to attune the body and bring it into a state of balance and equilibrium. Moreover, there are types of movement meditation known as tai-chi, yoga and qi-gong that also place a heavy emphasis on breath control. Not only does controlled breathing imrove your mental and emotional state it can also improve your physical well-being.

key points

  • Breathing is the fundamental basis of all meditation.
  • Breath-work helps to maximise the benefits of meditation.
  • Focusing on your breath while learning to meditate will help you to stay focused and present.
  • Pranic breathing delivers oxygen to every cell in your body.
  • There are movement meditation practices called yoga, tai-chi and qi-gong.

Focus

Many people can tell you from experience with meditation that meditation mingled with a consistent attitude improved their mental capacities over time. Especially, when it came to developing a mind that is able to focus for longer periods of time.

Among many of the meditation styles, mindfulness meditation in particular helps you to practice staying focused for longer periods by helping you to stay present. It is a type of brain conditioning that re-wires an easily distracted mind and makes your ability to focus laser sharp.

Attention span is an extremely important aspect to consider in todays world with the uprise of advanced technology and artificial intelligence. Putting aside time once or twice a day to sharpen your mental faculties is a must if you want to out do the negative influence of modern day technology.

Meditation can help you to stay focused in the most and at the least expected of times. The sharper your ability to focus becomes the more adept you become to noticing things in your environment that you may not have noticed otherwise. And there is also a possibility that these things will be of a spiritual nature.

key points

  • Meditation helps to increase your ability to focus for longer periods of time.
  • Mindfulness meditation in particualr is a type of re-conditioning of your mind.
  • A mind that is easily distracted can be reconditioned through regular practice of mindfulness meditation.

Time management

When people are first starting to learn meditation, one question they always have to ask is 'how long should I meditate for?'.

The first thing I would recommend is to tune in and listen to what your physical body is saying. Never allow yourself to become mentally fatigued. Don't sit for long periods if your body is not use to it.

When I first taught myself to meditate I would start with short periods and would find that my mind could not focus for periods longer then 10 seconds (yes, like a goldfish lol). Overtime as I became consistent with my practice ten seconds transformed into ten minutes. And ten minutes of mindfulness a day is sufficient for anyone learning how to integrate mindfulness into their daily routine.

key points

  • The body needs time to transition into a lifestyle of meditation from both a physical and psychological perspective.
  • Start with small time frames from anywhere in between 2- 5 mins then gradually increase your time limit as your mind and body become accustomed to meditation. In contrast, try not to limit yourself with timeframes as they could become a negative distraction.
  • A timer with an alarm set time limits for your self. Especially for those living on a tight schedule.

Desire

The world can be an overwhelming place sometimes and being able to take some time out of our busy lives and just breathe deeply can help us feel more relaxed about life’s challenges.

Mindfulness meditation allows us to see things from a different perspective – one where we are not so attached to outcomes or results but rather focused on what we have control over right now (our breathing ).

This helps us stay calm even when faced with difficult situations because we know that everything will pass eventually and there is nothing we can do about it anyway!

We learn how to let go of stress by practicing mindful living every day through simple activities like eating mindfully or taking deep breaths before responding emotionally during conflicts with loved ones/friends/colleagues etc.

How to integrate mindfulness into your life-style.

Research indicates that a vast majority of our behavior is influenced by automatic thinking. When we think we are doing something new, our brain is actually defaulting to old habits.

These default brain signals are so efficient that they often cause us to relapse into old behaviors before we remember what we were meant to do.

It’s unfortunate because these thoughts can be extremely detrimental for one’s health and wellness, but luckily, there is a way of stopping them in their tracks.

The more chances we get to activate the intentional brain the better. Putting meditation reminders around you and in your living environment remind you to be present.

Refreshing your reminders regularly helps to create new connections in the brain. This will help you to create new thinking patterns which help to boost creativity and problem solving.

If you really want to quiet your mind and be more creative the next time you’re trying to come up with a solution for your work-related problem, try using mindfulness as an aid.

By putting reminders around yourself (such as on a desk, in the kitchen, or on your phone screen), it will make it much easier to make mindfulness a regular habit.

Key points:

  • Habits are created through repetition: The more you do something the better you get at doing it. The aim is to ingrain the process of mindfulness into your psyche so that it becomes second nature. Regardless of where you are situated you will always be able to remain present and live a more fulfilling life.
  • Reminders never go astray: It is beneficial to be reminded of mindfulness throughout the day and even during your sleep. We are only human and it is very easy to forget about the little things as we go about our daily activities. More importantly, we should remember how easy it is to enter into a state of automatic thinking. The purpose of mindfulness meditation is to break the cycle to be present for a better quality of life.
  • Mindfulness meditation boosts creativity and problem solving: By learning to be present, we learn to be more aware of what is happening with regard to our thought processes. By allowing the mind to be still, we open our self up to receive external information from our surroundings. With the release of new solutions, we are able to come up with more creative and helpful ways to solve problems.

Obstacles for Mindfulness Meditation.

There are many obstacles which are presented when practicing mindfulness but they should not be seen as barriers but rather as growth opportunities which will help you overcome them. The key premise of mindfulness meditation is to keep bringing your mind back to the present moment. It’s about learning from your emotions during practice and then controlling them when you’re not practicing.

With mindfulness meditation, it is necessary to take on a new mind set. When we remove our attentional blinkers and ‘notice where they go’ it becomes easier to accept that reality doesn’t usually happen in a nice neat way but instead trickles into us gradually through waves of feedback loops between cognition, sensing, feeling.

Mindfulness meditation is a powerful tool for anyone who desires to find peace and serenity. It may take some time before you start feeling the effects, but once they come in full swing, you’ll be hooked. The obstacles that arise shouldn’t deter you; rather, see them as growth opportunities that will only make your practice stronger with time!

Key Points:

  • Distractions are opportunities for growth:

Take note of distractions and notice the way your emotions respond to them. Notice the thought patterns that arise in your mind and how they contribute to your emotional state. Do you feel agitated? Irritated? Restless? Does your ability to focus subside? Is your response to the distraction/s a healthy one? Remember, being mindful is not about controlling ourselves and the world around us, it’s about being present and immersed in the moment.
