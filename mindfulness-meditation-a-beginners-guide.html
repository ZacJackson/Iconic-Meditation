By Zac Jackson

Meditation 101: Getting to know the basics.

Comfortability

Sit in a seated position with your legs crossed or both feet flat on the floor. This will help to prevent you from falling asleep and with keeping your spine in an erect position (if that is possible). If you have issues with keeping your spine straight then it is also ok to sit or lie in a position where you feel as comfortable as you can.

key points

  • Wear comfortable clothing.
  • Sit in a comfortable position.

Environment

Find a comfortable place to sit where you won’t be distracted. And bare in mind that thoughts and physical sensations, such as an itch can be distracting. Overtime as you get better at meditating small distractions become anchors which will help you to progress with the practice of meditation.

Overtime as you begin to master mindfulness meditation you will find it easier to meditate in, and around the presence of noise. Remember that you have an internal environment as well as an external environment. Our aim is to work on our internal environment in order for our mind’s to change the way we interact with the external environment.

key points

  • Sit somewhere that helps you to relax
  • Sit where distractions are minimal.
  • Your internal environment determines your external one.

Breathing

Take a deep breath in slowly through your nostrils until you feel your diaphragm fill with oxygen (prana). Slowly exhale through your mouth. Let out as much air as you can but do not force it.

Let it flow naturally. Repeat this exercise 3-5 times until you feel relaxed. Once you have completed 3-5 cycles allow yourself to breathe normally. You can achieve this by bringing your attention away from the breath and into the mind to observe.

Notice how certain thoughts influence the rate at which you breathe. Some increase your heart rate and others slow it down. Stay focused on observing thoughts and feelings rather then attaching to them. Breathe naturally and try to stay relaxed.

Come back to the breath when your mind drifts. Utilize deep breathing to bring your self into a relaxed state and back into the present moment. A calm mind produces positive results.

key points

  • Breathe all the way in until your diaphragm fills with air (prana) then exhale through your nose.
  • Breathe deeply until your mind is relaxed then start to let your breathing happen naturally.
  • Stay focused on breathing as you inhale and exhale.
  • Focus on your breath which will help you to be present and in the moment.


Focus

Pay very close attention to your breath as you inhale and exhale. Notice the air going in and out. As you draw your attention towards yourself breathing notice the thoughts coming into the forefront of your mind. Pay attention to how these thoughts make you feel.

Acknowledge your emotional state. Let them arise then subside. Refrain from attaching to any thought that causes your emotions to charge. Just observe and become aware of the patterns in your mind.

Notice any physical sensation that occurs. If you need to scratch and itch then feel free to scratch the itch. Alternatively, you may let the sensation go away at its own will. Be mindful that it takes practice to achieve that level of awareness.

key points

  • Your mind will drift countless times. Keep coming back to the present moment by focusing on your breath (or something that your senses are picking up).
  • Observe your thoughts, emotions and physical sensations. Notice the patterns in your mind regarding thoughts and emotions.
  • Observe thoughts rather then attaching to, and thinking about them.


Time management

Whether it’s for ten seconds or 10 hours, being mindful will always bring you into the present moment away from the habit of drifting.

Trust the process and try not attach to an outcome. Mindfulness can be practiced anytime and anywhere. Consistency is the key so regular practice, even a few minutes a day can have a profound impact on your life.

With time comes growth. Your mind and body have been through a lot in the past. Be kind to yourself. It is very easy to become frustrated and give up. Start with small 5-10 second bouts of mental silence over 3-5 minute periods throughout your day. Do this at least 2-3 times a day until you’re ready to take it further.

  • Keep bringing your focus back to your breathing whenever your mind drifts.
  • Practice for 3-5minutes a day until you are comfortable extending the time limit.
  • Be consistent and you will reap the reward of a more fulfilling and meaningful life.

Attention

If you’re interested in mindfulness meditation, I recommend looking into the benefits of using guided meditations and other mindfulness techniques.

Interest

Mindfulness meditation is a simple practice that you can do at any time. You don’t need to be sitting in the lotus position on top of a mountain out in the wilderness; all you need is 5 minutes per day to start reaping the rewards it offers!

Overall you’ll be able to reduce stress and improve your health by practicing mindfulness meditation regularly.

Desire

The world can be an overwhelming place sometimes and being able to take some time out of our busy lives and just breathe deeply can help us feel more relaxed about life’s challenges.

Mindfulness meditation allows us to see things from a different perspective – one where we are not so attached to outcomes or results but rather focused on what we have control over right now (our breathing).

This helps us stay calm even when faced with difficult situations because we know that everything will pass eventually and there is nothing we can do about it anyway!

We learn how to let go of stress by practicing mindful living every day through simple activities like eating mindfully or taking deep breaths before responding emotionally during conflicts with loved ones/friends/colleagues etc.

Action

Taking action now is a sure way to improve your well-being and overall health. Get started today. Don't let thoughts and emotions hold you back from reaching your full potential.

How to integrate mindfulness into your life.

Research indicates that a vast majority of our behavior is influenced by automatic thinking. When we think we are doing something new, our brain is actually defaulting to old habits.

These default brain signals are so efficient that they often cause us to relapse into old behaviors before we remember what we were meant to do.

It’s unfortunate because these thoughts can be extremely detrimental for one’s health and wellness, but luckily, there is a way of stopping them in their tracks.

The more chances we get to activate the intentional brain the better. Putting meditation reminders around you and in your living environment remind you to be present.

Refreshing your reminders regularly helps to create new connections in the brain. This will help you to create new thinking patterns which help to boost creativity and problem solving.

If you really want to quiet your mind and be more creative the next time you’re trying to come up with a solution for your work-related problem, try using mindfulness as an aid.

By putting reminders around yourself (such as on a desk, in the kitchen, or on your phone screen), it will make it much easier to make mindfulness a regular habit.

Key points:

  • Habits are created through repetition: The more you do something the better you get at doing it. The aim is to ingrain the process of mindfulness into your psyche so that it becomes second nature. Regardless of where you are situated you will always be able to remain present and live a more fulfilling life.
  • Reminders never go astray: It is beneficial to be reminded of mindfulness throughout the day and even during your sleep. We are only human and it is very easy to forget about the little things as we go about our daily activities. More importantly, we should remember how easy it is to enter into a state of automatic thinking. The purpose of mindfulness meditation is to break that cycle and to be present for a better quality of life.
  • Mindfulness meditation boosts creativity and problem solving: By learning to be present, we learn to be more aware of what is happening with regard to our thought processes. By allowing the mind to be still, we open our self up to receive external information from our surroundings. With the release of new solutions, we are able to come up with more creative and helpful ways to solve problems.

Obstacles for Mindfulness Meditation.

There are many obstacles which are presented when practicing mindfulness but they should not be seen as barriers but rather as growth opportunities which will help you overcome them. The key premise of mindfulness meditation is to keep bringing your mind back to the present moment. It’s about learning from your emotions during practice and then controlling them when you’re not practicing.

With mindfulness meditation, it is necessary to take on a new mind set. When we remove our attentional blinkers and ‘notice where they go’ it becomes easier to accept that reality doesn’t usually happen in a nice neat way but instead trickles into us gradually through waves of feedback loops between cognition, sensing, feeling.

Mindfulness meditation is a powerful tool for anyone who desires to find peace and serenity. It may take some time before you start feeling the effects, but once they come in full swing, you’ll be hooked. The obstacles that arise shouldn’t deter you; rather, see them as growth opportunities that will only make your practice stronger with time!

Key Points:

  • Distractions are opportunities for growth:

Take note of distractions and notice the way your emotions respond to them. Notice the thought patterns that arise in your mind and how they contribute to your emotional state. Do you feel agitated? Irritated? Restless? Does your ability to focus subside? Is your response to the distraction/s a healthy one? Remember, being mindful is not about controlling ourselves and the world around us, it’s about being present and immersed in the moment.
